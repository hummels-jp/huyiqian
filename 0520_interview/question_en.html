08_Gyosei Kindergarten Interview Prep

This document contains the interview questions prepared by JAC for "Gyosei Kindergarten," along with suggested answers based on your family's background.

Father's Section

  • Please tell us your reason for applying.
    I deeply respect Gyosei's long history, traditions, and rigorous academic environment, which I believe form the best foundation for a child's growth. As an IT Project Manager, I strongly identify with Gyosei's emphasis on character building. I hope my son can grow up in the nurturing environment of Gyosei's Catholic teachings, becoming a modern gentleman who is not only internationally minded but also possesses a peaceful, dedicated, and resilient heart.
  • What is your view on the school's educational policy?
    I find the combination of "holistic education" and "discipline" exceptional. Gyosei goes beyond academic knowledge, focusing on cultivating cooperation and responsibility through collective life, which aligns perfectly with our family's philosophy of "self-discipline and confidence."
  • (Catholic school) Do you have any concerns regarding religious education? Are you willing to cooperate?
    We have no concerns. We deeply identify with the teachings of Catholicism as symbols of peace and love. As parents, we are very willing to cooperate with all school religious activities, as this will provide a valuable foundation for our child's spiritual growth.
  • Is choosing a religious school the father's or mother's choice?
    It is a mutual decision reached after careful consideration. We believe Gyosei's values provide the best spiritual support to prevent our child from feeling lost in modern society.
  • When should a father step in (deep participation)?
    When the child needs to establish boundaries, understand teamwork, or face setbacks. I use weekends for skiing and swimming to teach him about rules and challenges, guiding him through fatherhood to learn responsibility.
  • Are there any daily habits you are concerned about?
    Currently, I am focused on his "sense of placement." Though very active, his focus is easily diverted by his Ultraman figures; I am guiding him to manage them through categorization to build better organization skills.
  • Difference between "Affection" and "Spoiling"?
    Affection is empowering by providing security; spoiling is disempowering by stripping away boundaries. As a father, I allow space for exploration but remain firm on principles and habit formation.
  • What games do you prioritize during early childhood?
    I prioritize rule-based and creative games. For example, role-playing Ultraman cultivates his sense of justice, and outdoor sports teach him respect for rules and physical coordination.
  • What child growth brings you the most joy?
    Seeing the confidence in his eyes when he independently overcomes a small difficulty (like mastering breathing in swimming) brings me immense joy.
  • Do you read picture books to your child?
    Yes, I often read scientific books about dinosaurs with him in three languages, which serves as both knowledge building and high-quality parent-child interaction.
  • What is the child's challenge (improvement area)?
    He is a bit "slow to warm up" (cautious in new environments). However, we encourage him to share his expertise in dinosaurs with peers, helping him gradually expand his social boundaries.
  • Does the child attend any extracurricular classes?
    Yes, skiing and swimming. These build perseverance and align with our goal of fostering a courageous personality.
  • Your thoughts on raising a boy?
    I hope to raise a "strong yet warm-hearted" gentleman. Not just knowledgeable, but someone with a sense of compassion for the weak and resilience in the face of setbacks.
  • (If mother filled out survey) Have you heard the content? What do you think?
    We communicated thoroughly. I fully agree with my wife's perspective, and we work as a highly aligned team in child-rearing.
  • (If siblings) Differences in raising boys vs girls?
    I teach according to each child's individual talent while ensuring high-quality, dedicated time for each, avoiding a "one size fits all" approach.
  • (If siblings, age gap) Strengths of the youngest?
    Strong observation skills and the ability to learn from siblings while possessing a unique creative perspective.
  • (If siblings) Do you treat them differently?
    Yes, I respect individual differences and provide guidance suitable for each.
  • (If siblings) How do you spend time despite being busy?
    I ensure a fixed "high-quality companionship time" daily, ensuring I participate in their growth and interaction regardless of work demands.
  • (If alumni) Still in contact with classmates?
    Yes, we keep close ties; those friendships are a priceless treasure.
  • (If alumni) Best benefit of graduating from this school?
    Gyosei endowed me with lifelong analytical thinking and an alumni network that remains a cornerstone of my progress in the IT industry.

Mother's Section

  • Can the mother participate actively in school activities?
    Absolutely. I have coordinated my work schedule to make school events a family priority and look forward to supporting my child's growth.
  • Can you handle emergencies?
    Yes. We have a clear contingency plan to ensure immediate response to emergencies.
  • How does the child spend time after school hours (13:30)?
    Mainly reading books, outdoor sports, or participating in exploratory scientific experiments.
  • Are you okay with the all-boy environment in elementary school?
    I am very confident. An all-boy environment fosters competition and cooperation, helping him build a courageous, independent, and responsible character.
  • Thoughts on the child's personality?
    He has strong logic and is focused, despite being slow to warm up. This deliberateness is his potential advantage for future challenges.
  • Any extracurriculars?
    Skiing and swimming.
  • Challenges to improve?
    Currently a bit shy in new groups, but we are guiding him to open up through collective activities.
  • Do they play pretend games?
    Yes, he loves acting as Ultraman or an explorer, which is a display of his logic and creativity.
  • (Working mother) Tell us about your work situation.
    I ensure sufficient time and energy are dedicated to child-rearing while balancing my career.
  • How do you guide the child when he takes too long or time is tight?
    I use task-oriented methods, setting small milestones and building confidence through small achievements.
  • How do you teach tidying up?
    I emphasize "sense of placement" and gamify the process, letting him enjoy the order of things returning to their rightful places.
  • How do husband and child interact at home?
    They are sports partners and knowledge exploration buddies. They often research dinosaur facts together, enjoying deep and warm interactions.
